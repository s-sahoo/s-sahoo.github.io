    Fab 23, 2022

    Simple and Effective Masked Diffusion Language Models

    While diffusion models excel at generating high-quality images, they have traditionally lagged behind autoregressive (AR) methods in language modeling. This work demonstrates that masked discrete diffusion is more effective than previously thought. By applying an effective training recipe and a simplified, Rao-Blackwellized objective, we improve the performance of masked diffusion models. These models, trained with modern practices, achieve a new state-of-the-art among diffusion models and approach AR perplexity on language modeling benchmarks.

    Fab 23, 2022

    Diffusion Models With Learned Adaptive Noise

    Diffusion models are powerful for synthesizing high-quality images, but their performance depends heavily on the diffusion process. This paper investigates whether the diffusion process can be learned from data, grounded in Bayesian inference to improve log-likelihood estimation. The proposed method, multivariate learned adaptive noise (MuLAN), applies noise at different rates across an image, dispelling the assumption that the ELBO is invariant to the noise process. Empirically, MuLAN achieves state-of-the-art density estimation on CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, reducing training steps by 50%.
